What is Henna?…Some of you may ask!


Henna is actually a plant! The leaves of a henna plant contain a natural red dye called “Lawsone”. For many many generations this dye has been used to create beautiful body art. It is a rich tradition that is shared amongst various cultures around the world such as South East Asia, Africa, and Middle East.

The leaves are taken from the henna plant, dried, and then grounded to make a fine powder. The powder is then sifted thoroughly and mixed with a variety of ingredients such as lemon, sugar, water, and essential oils to create a smooth paste. This paste is then poured into an applicator so that the artist can create precise lines and designs. Once the henna is applied to the skin and it has fully dried, it can then be removed from the skin by gently scraping it off. It doesn’t take too long for the henna to dry, but it is highly recommended to keep the paste on for as long as you can for the darkest possible stain! After the removal, it is also recommended to keep the skin dry for at least 6-8 hours. You are left with a deep red beautiful stain that adorns your skin for about a couple of weeks! The best part about Henna is that once the henna wears off, you can try a new design!

About me:

Hi! My name is Kam. I am a New York based henna artist with over 15 years of experience. I am experienced in both traditional and modern henna designs. I love taking different styles of henna art from around the world such as Indian, African, Moroccan, and Arabic designs to create unique body art. My inspiration comes from everything around me, from the architecture around me to sacred geometric patterns I see everywhere.

The art of henna has been a passion for me since I was 12. I remember it was Eid (kind of like Christmas for people of the Islamic faith) when I first started doodling on myself. On this holiday (and many other occasions such as weddings, birthdays, and just about any festive celebrations), it’s tradition to adorn our hands and feet with henna. That year I wanted to do my own henna. I realized it came naturally to me. Since then I have been doing henna for myself, family, and friends. I fell in love with the art of henna. I love the feeling of mixing the henna with my hands, the unique smell of the plant paste, and the excitement of creating designs and seeing people’s reactions when they see the beautiful stains. As I grew more confident in my work, I wanted to turn my passion into a business and share my love for henna!
